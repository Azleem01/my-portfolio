ABOUT

Data, models, and things that actually work

I'm a data scientist and machine learning engineer who cares about the gap between a model in a notebook and a tool people can trust. My work runs from classic ML and data apps to LLM-powered products and adversarial AI safety.

As technical lead on Sentinel AI, I built the full stack of an emergency-department triage decision-support prototype for the Imperial College Global Challenge Lab (Health & Technology track): synthetic-data model training, a FastAPI scoring service, and a React triage board, all deliberately scoped and documented as a prototype, not a medical device.

I like building things that are honest about their limits: shipped apps with graceful fallbacks, research artifacts that show where protection breaks down, and models with held-out metrics you can check.
